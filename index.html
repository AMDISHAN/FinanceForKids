Lesson 1: What is Money?

Money is an essential part of our everyday life. It is used to buy goods (things like food, clothes, and toys) and services (things like haircuts, movies, and a ride on the bus). Money comes in many forms: coins, bills, and digital currency. It's important to understand that money is not endless; it needs to be managed wisely to ensure we can use it when we need it.

Every time we need something, we exchange money for it. If we run out of money, we can’t buy what we want or need. That’s why saving and budgeting are so important. Money can come from many places, like allowances, jobs, or gifts. But no matter where it comes from, learning how to use money properly is a very important skill!

What is money used for?

To buy goods and services
To store food
To play games

Lesson 2: Budgeting Your Money

Budgeting is a way of organizing and planning how you will use your money. A budget helps you make sure you don't spend too much, and it shows you how much you can save. The first step in budgeting is knowing how much money you have, and the second step is deciding how to spend it wisely.

For example, imagine you get $10 for your weekly allowance. You might need to pay for a snack, save for a new toy, or even save for a trip. You must decide how to spend your $10 so you have enough to cover your needs, and still have some leftover for saving or fun stuff!

Remember: budgeting isn't about cutting out fun. It's about being smart and making sure you have money when you need it. It’s a simple way to make sure you always have enough for your most important things!

Why do we need a budget?

To plan how to spend and save money
To spend all your money quickly
To borrow money from friends

Lesson 3: Saving Money

Saving money means keeping part of your money instead of spending it right away. Saving is important because it helps you plan for the future. When you save, you put aside money for something big you want later, like a new game or bike, or for things you might need, like school supplies or gifts.

Imagine you want a new toy that costs $20. If you only have $10 right now, you can save $2 every week. After 5 weeks, you'll have enough! Saving also teaches you to wait and plan for what you really want instead of spending all your money on things that might not be as important.

Saving money helps you feel secure because you know you have money set aside when you need it, whether it's for a rainy day or a fun treat!

Why is saving money important?

It helps you prepare for the future
It lets you spend all your money now
It’s only for adults

Lesson 4: Investing: Growing Your Money

Investing is when you put your money into something that can help it grow. Instead of just saving your money, you can invest it in things like stocks, bonds, or even a business. The idea is that, over time, your money will increase in value.

Think about planting a seed in a garden. If you take care of it, it will grow into a tree. Similarly, if you invest your money wisely, it can grow and become more valuable over time. Investing is not without risks, though. Sometimes, investments can lose value. It’s important to learn about where and how to invest wisely.

Investing can be fun, especially when you start learning about companies and how they make money. It’s one of the ways people grow their wealth and save for their long-term goals!

What is investing?

Using money to make more money over time

Giving away all your money

Lesson 5: How to Earn Money

To manage money, you first need to earn it! There are many ways to earn money: through an allowance, by doing chores, or even by starting a small business like a lemonade stand or selling crafts. Earning money helps you learn the value of hard work.

When you earn money, it gives you a sense of accomplishment. You get to decide how to use it, whether it's for saving, spending, or giving to others. Learning how to earn money and manage it is one of the first steps in becoming financially independent.

What is one way to earn money?

Doing chores or a small job
Taking someone else’s money
Asking for gifts all the time

Lesson 6: Understanding Credit

Credit is when you borrow money with the promise to pay it back later. It’s used for things like buying a car or a house, but it’s important to understand how credit works before you use it.

When you borrow money using credit, you must pay it back with interest, which means you pay more than you borrowed. If you don’t pay back the money on time, it can damage your credit score, which makes it harder to borrow money in the future. Being responsible with credit is key!

What does credit allow you to do?

Borrow money to pay back later
Keep money without paying it back
Spend money without worrying

Lesson 7: Taxes and How They Work

Taxes are money that people pay to the government to help pay for things like schools, roads, and emergency services. Every time you buy something, a small amount of money is added as tax. This is called sales tax.

When adults work, a portion of their earnings is also taken as tax to fund government services. It's important to understand taxes because they help provide services that benefit everyone.

What are taxes used for?

To fund services like schools and roads
To buy new toys
To give away free money

Lesson 8: Planning for the Future

Planning for the future is one of the most important financial skills you can learn. It involves thinking ahead and deciding how you want to spend, save, and invest your money. You may not always know exactly what will happen, but setting goals and planning for different scenarios will help you be ready for whatever comes your way.

One way to plan for the future is by thinking about what you might need or want, such as going to college or buying your first car. These things require money, so the earlier you start planning and saving, the better prepared you’ll be. Saving and budgeting now means you’ll be ready for your future!

What does planning for the future help you do?

Prepare for big life events
Spend all your money quickly
Forget about your goals

Lesson 9: Charity and Giving Back

Charity is when you give some of your money or time to help others who need it. Giving is a great way to make a positive difference in the world and help those who might be less fortunate. You can donate money to causes you care about or volunteer to help in your community.

Learning to give back teaches you empathy and kindness. It also helps you realize that while it’s important to manage your own money, it’s just as important to think about how you can use your resources to help others.

What is charity?

Helping others by giving money or time
Spending all your money on yourself
Ignoring the needs of others

Lesson 10: Entrepreneurship

Entrepreneurship is when you start your own business to sell products or services. It’s about being creative, solving problems, and taking risks. Many people start small businesses at a young age, like selling lemonade, creating art to sell, or offering to mow lawns for neighbors.

Starting a business teaches you about responsibility, hard work, and the importance of providing something valuable to others. Even though starting a business can be hard, it can also be very rewarding!

What is entrepreneurship?

Starting your own business
